# MoXY 3D Printer

MoXY is a custom FDM cross-gantry 3D printer, fully designed and built from scratch.

{% include image-pair.html left="assets/MoXY_cad.jpg" alt_left="MoXY CAD Model" right="assets/MoXY.jpg" alt_right="Current Build Progress" %}

---

## Motivation

Development began after a professor remarked that 3D printing was too slow to be viable for iterative concept design. Rather than accepting that constraint, I set out to design a machine that challenged it — one capable of high-speed printing with exotic materials at sustained chamber temperatures above 120°C.

Work started in Summer 2024, with a focus on combining precision, speed, and material versatility in a single platform. The frame is constructed entirely from steel, chosen to eliminate the differential thermal expansion problems common in steel-rail-on-aluminum-beam designs.

## Motion System

MoXY uses a cross-gantry configuration with four stepper motors driving each of the X and Y axes simultaneously. Recent developments in stepper synchronization allow multiple motors to share a single belt, which increases available torque while keeping individual belt lengths short. Shorter belts raise the resonant frequency of belt-induced vibrations, pushing them outside the range that affects print quality at high accelerations.

The bearings and shaft geometry required careful analysis due to the elevated belt tensions involved. **Needle bearings** were selected for their distributed load capacity, and shaft deflection was calculated using:

$$
\delta_{max} = \frac{Fb \left( L^2 - b^2 \right)^{3/2}}{9 \sqrt{3} L E I}
$$

These calculations confirmed deflection remained within acceptable tolerances. Linear rails are mounted on a monolithic ground steel plate, providing the parallelism required by the motion system — an approach consistent with high-end platforms such as the [Pantheon HS3](https://www.pantheondesign.com/3d-printer).

## Custom Extruder

The extruder was designed from the ground up to handle the torque and heat demands of high-speed printing with engineering-grade materials. It uses a **15:1 worm gear reduction** and an all-metal construction, with components manufactured via **SLM 3D printing** in 316L stainless steel to achieve the required geometry while maintaining structural rigidity.

The metal body passively dissipates heat generated by both the extruder motor and the hotend, and its stiffness allows higher accelerations without the compliance issues present in lighter extruder designs.

## Heated Bed

Thermal expansion of the build plate during heat-up presents a significant positioning challenge. A **Maxwell kinematic coupling** was implemented at the bed mounting points, which constrains the bed to a deterministic position while allowing free thermal expansion in all directions. This eliminates thermally induced stress and preserves bed location repeatability across the full operating temperature range.

## Electronics

MoXY operates on a split-voltage architecture: 24V for general peripherals and 48V for the high-torque X/Y stepper motors. Motion planning runs on **Klipper** hosted on a Raspberry Pi, providing WiFi connectivity and a flexible configuration layer. Several distributed temperature probes monitor chamber conditions, and custom software manages fan control, relay switching for the 110V heated bed and chamber heater, and hotend temperature regulation.

All electronics are housed in a purpose-built enclosure with modular DIN rail mounting and custom wiring to simplify maintenance and future modifications.

## Specs

*Note: Some specifications are projected values based on comparable machines, as the printer is still under construction.*

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Build Volume | 300 × 300 × 250 mm |
| Heated Bed | Up to 120°C sustained |
| Print Acceleration | 60,000 mm/s² |
| Max Volumetric Flow | 80 mm³/s (0.4 mm nozzle) |
| Extruder | All-metal worm gear, 15:1 reduction |

---

## Manufacturing Methods

- CAD designed in **SolidWorks**
- Flat structural parts: **waterjet** and **manual mill**
- Complex geometry: outsourced **SLM 3D printing** (316L stainless)
- Structural prototypes: **FDM 3D printing**
- CNC machined parts produced from engineering drawings with GD&T callouts
- Electronics: **soldering**, **crimping**, and custom wiring harnesses
