# Boulder Ball
**UC Berkeley** | *April 2023*

The Boulder Ball is a portable climbing training tool designed around an icosahedron (20-sided polyhedron) with fully interchangeable holds, allowing the route to be reconfigured on demand.

---

## Design

The project was modeled in **Onshape** as an opportunity to explore CAD software outside of my usual toolset. The icosahedron serves as the structural base, with threaded inserts embedded into each face to securely mount modular holds. Over ten unique hold designs were created, each with varying grip geometry and texture to replicate different difficulty levels.

During prototyping, several usability issues were identified and resolved. Face numbering was added as indented geometry on each panel to allow routes to be communicated and repeated. An allen key holder recessed into the body and retained by a magnet was integrated so that holds can be swapped without carrying a separate tool.

---

## Fabrication

Slicing parameters were optimized to balance impact resistance with overall weight — the final assembly is robust enough to survive drops while light enough to be carried in one hand. Hold surfaces were modified to include a textured outer layer, significantly improving grip with sweaty hands.
